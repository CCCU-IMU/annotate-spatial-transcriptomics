# Sheep ovary profile evidence

## Evidence weighting

Weight a source by directness to the current question before journal prestige: whole-ovary coverage, sheep specificity, matched adult/developmental stage, observation unit, cell count, marker validation and spatial/histological support determine taxonomic weight. A high-impact paper restricted to purified oocytes, cumulus cells or one mechanism can refine a state/module but cannot define the whole-ovary broad-class tree.

### Tier A: whole-ovary sheep taxonomy and lineage boundaries

- A 2026 *Advanced Science* sheep–human reproductive/CNS atlas contains more than 1.09 million cells across 15 sheep tissues and supports conserved germline, stromal, epithelial, immune and endothelial lineages plus granulosa/theca/oocyte reproductive programs. It is a high-level cross-tissue conservation reference, not a single-section class quota. [PMID 41566606](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41566606/), DOI `10.1002/advs.202517633`.
- A 2026 *Journal of Animal Science and Biotechnology* analysis integrated 186,748 ovarian cells/nuclei from nine vertebrate species and recovered nine cell types, with conserved granulosa and stromal programs. Its sheep component reuses adult GSE233801, so it is strong boundary/conservation evidence but not an independent sheep discovery cohort. [PMID 41975518](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41975518/), DOI `10.1186/s40104-026-01383-1`.
- The 2024 *Molecular Biology and Evolution* Tibetan-sheep multiomics study profiled 111,548 ovarian cells and released seven broad types: stromal (44.14%), granulosa (28.73%), oocyte (3.77%), endothelial (8.73%), epithelial (7.81%), immune (4.85%) and perivascular (1.96%). It did not require separate Theca, Mesenchymal or Smooth muscle broad labels, demonstrating that those categories are evidence-dependent rather than mandatory. [PMID 38552245](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38552245/), DOI `10.1093/molbev/msae058`.
- The 2025 *iScience* developmental atlas profiled 61,649 cells at E90, M3, M6, Y2 and Y4 and reported nine major classes: stromal, granulosa, oocyte, immune, epithelial, endothelial, theca, mesenchymal and smooth muscle. Theca used `CYP11A1/LHCGR`; Smooth muscle used `MYH11/MYL9/TAGLN`; only 280 cells were called Mesenchymal using `S100A4`. Oocytes occurred only in E90 and Y2, underscoring sampling/stage effects. [PMID 40641558](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40641558/), DOI `10.1016/j.isci.2025.112422`.
- The 2023 *Journal of Animal Science and Biotechnology* adult-estrus Hu-sheep study/GSE233801 profiled 38,921 cells, defined endothelial, granulosa, stromal, perivascular and immune somatic classes, and left DCDC2/MUC16, MPZ/CDH19 and MZB1/VPREB3 clusters unknown. This is valuable evidence that honest unknown/review states and a shallow adult taxonomy are preferable to forced completion. [PMID 37964337](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37964337/), DOI `10.1186/s40104-023-00941-1`, [GEO](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE233801).
- A 2026 *FASEB Journal* whole-ovary comparison of high-prolificacy Hu and low-prolificacy Mongolian sheep identified eight major clusters and experimentally examined macrophage–granulosa IGFBP2/IGF2 crosstalk. Use it to strengthen the Immune–Granulosa boundary and macrophage module, not to require an immune subtype in every spatial section. [PMID 41801067](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41801067/), DOI `10.1096/fj.202502486RR`.

### Tier A2: recent cross-species whole-ovary boundary evidence

- The 2025 *Science* human–mouse whole-ovary study recovered Oocyte, Granulosa, Theca, Fibroblast, Smooth muscle, Pericyte, Epithelia, Endothelia, Immune and rare Glia as broad candidates, with Luteal cells in the sampled mouse state. Broad identities were more conserved than theca, pericyte and epithelial fine states. Glia required coherent `S100B/SOX10`-like evidence and nerve-associated morphology; the paper did not recover neurons by ovary scRNA-seq. Use this to audit rare/contractile boundaries, not to force cross-species labels. DOI `10.1126/science.adx0659`.
- The 2025 AJOG adult-ovary expert review identifies recurrent Oocyte, Granulosa, Theca, Stroma, Endothelial, Smooth muscle, Perivascular and Immune classes while emphasizing sampling/filtration loss, fragile surface epithelium, single-marker weakness and effectively endless computational subtyping. This supports shallow release labels and explicit negative audits. DOI `10.1016/j.ajog.2024.05.046`.

### Tier B: targeted sheep lineage/state references

- The 2024 *Scientific Reports* FecB study profiled tens of thousands of purified/superstimulated ovine cumulus cells and supports real cumulus-state heterogeneity and oxidative-phosphorylation variation. It cannot by itself label a whole-section Granulosa subtype. [PMID 38849498](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38849498/), DOI `10.1038/s41598-024-64001-y`.
- Sheep single-oocyte studies resolve follicle-size, competence, atresia and maturation states. Use their non-ZP maternal/ooplasm programs to validate true Oocyte candidates, but do not transfer their Type 1/2/3 or follicle-size states to cellbins lacking a strict oocyte core and object morphology. Relevant examples include [PMID 38255985](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38255985/), [PMID 39233000](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39233000/), [PMID 39893388](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39893388/) and [PMID 42272526](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42272526/).

## Operational synthesis

- Without a usable matched count-level reference, GSE233801 is the primary public adult-sheep somatic atlas. It provides anchors for endothelial, granulosa, stromal, perivascular and immune populations, but its authors retain several unknown groups; it is not a sufficient Oocyte/Theca/Epithelial answer source. A matched marker dotplot does not change this priority because it cannot calibrate cell-level transfer. [GEO](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE233801), [Ge et al. 2023](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40104-023-00941-1).
- GSE233801 reports five somatic lineages and a limited granulosa subtype analysis. Use it to justify a small functional granulosa vocabulary, not to give every query cluster a subtype name. Its absent/unknown populations are evidence against forced completion.
- The 2025 sheep ovarian-development atlas supports developmental Oocyte, Theca, Epithelial and major somatic programs and explicitly shows stage/sampling dependence. [Zhao et al., iScience 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12245445/).
- The same sheep atlas reports nine major classes. It defines Smooth muscle with high `MYH11/MYL9/TAGLN` and calls only 280 of 61,649 cells Mesenchymal using `S100A4`. Therefore Smooth muscle deserves an explicit mature-contractile audit, whereas a standalone Mesenchymal label must not be manufactured from generic `DCN/LUM/COL1A1` stroma. A missing standalone Mesenchymal label is not automatically an annotation failure; failure to test and record the distinction is. [Zhao et al., iScience 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12245445/).
- Human ovary spatial/single-cell work supports cortex/medulla differences and the oocyte → cumulus granulosa → mural granulosa → theca follicular architecture. [Jones et al., Science Advances 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10997207/).
- The human atlas separates dominant `DCN/PDGFRA/LUM` stromal cells, `RGS5/NOTCH3/ACTA2/PDGFRB` pericytes and SMA-enriched myofibroblast/smooth-muscle regions. This supports an explicit stromal-lineage decomposition rather than automatic merging or automatic creation of a Mesenchymal class. [Jones et al., Science Advances 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10997207/).
- Cross-species ovarian analysis uses `DCN/LUM/COL1A1` for stromal, `TAGLN/RGS5/ACTA2` for perivascular, `CDH5/VWF` for endothelial and steroidogenic enzymes for theca, supporting multi-program anti-marker gates. [Cross-species ovarian atlas 2026](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC13077819/).
- Mature ovarian smooth muscle should be separated from pericyte and myofibroblast/structural-theca only when mature contractile genes such as `MYH11/CNN1/ACTG2` accompany `TAGLN/ACTA2/MYL9/DES` and coherent morphology. Adult human ovarian atlases independently report smooth-muscle and pericyte/vascular-SM compartments. [Lengyel et al. atlas](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11295111/).
- High-density spatial bins can form mixed oocyte–pregranulosa populations; spatial oocyte signal is not automatically an independent cell identity. [Mouse Visium-HD ovary 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12458305/).
- Theca and stroma form biologically continuous programs; steroidogenic modules and follicular outer-ring morphology are needed to avoid overcalling Theca from ECM/LHCGR alone. [Theca/stroma trajectory study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9812973/).
- A `DLG2/RBFOX1/TENM3`-like signal without `SOX10/S100B/PLP1/MPZ` glial or `CHGA/CHGB/SYP/SCG/INSM1` neuroendocrine backbones cannot create a Neural/Schwann or Neuroendocrine class. Record a neural-like state under the resident lineage when full-program and morphology gates fail. [Gaylord et al., Science 2025](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.adx0659).

Use these sources to define priors and evidence gates. Do not import their final labels or numerical thresholds into a query without validation.

The cross-study consensus core for an adult whole-ovary spatial sample is a **candidate audit backbone**, not a required output: `Granulosa`, `Stromal/mesenchymal`, `Vascular/endothelial`, `Immune`, `Epithelial/mesothelial` and strict `Oocyte`. The complete machine-actionable catalog additionally audits steroidogenic Theca/luteal, mature Smooth muscle, Pericyte/mural, Mesenchymal progenitor-like, lymphatic endothelial, Neural/Schwann/glia and Neuroendocrine boundaries. These additions are evidence-, stage- or anatomy-dependent, and a negative audit is valid. The catalog is non-exhaustive: a coherent current-query program absent from it must still create an additional-candidate record. The literature does not support using `Theca/follicular wall` or `Stromal/perivascular` as convenient final catch-all labels.

Subtype restraint is part of biological accuracy. The 2025 multi-stage sheep atlas can resolve more states because it integrates 61,649 single cells across five ages; a single adult spatial section should not inherit all of those subtype names. For spatial cellbins, retain a broad label and state tag unless a subtype has a complete positive program, negative-program clearance, adjacent-resolution stability and compatible morphology.
